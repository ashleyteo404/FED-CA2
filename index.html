About Me

According to the  , I'm an ENFP.

Welcome to my website :D

Before I explain what it means to be an ENFP according to 16personalities, let me first introduce you to 16personalities. So, the 16 personalities test is based off the Myers-Briggs Theory that everyone can be classified into 16 personality types, which, as you can imagine, isn't the most accurate. However, it does still count for something! Unfortunately, the 16personalities website is known for being inaccurate in typing people as unlike other personality tests based off this theory, it types you by type (as in the individual letter) instead of by function (in simple terms, how the letters interact with each other to form your cognitive functions). Despite this, I chose to use the 16personalities test as it can be a good starting point for understanding typology in a way that the general public can consume given that it is typing by type instead of function is more well-known and simpler.

Click on the arrows to see the individual types!

Disclaimer: I am not a professional.

E - Extraverted

"Extraverts are interested in engaging with their environment, and they feed off the responses of the people and events around them. They enjoy pushing limits and seeing what the world can do." ~ 16personalities

It's true. I find that my energy tends to have a lot of input and output. In other words, majority of the time, I will express my energy. I wear my heart on my sleeve! Actually...I was advised to never gamble or play in a casino because my lack of a poker face would cause me to lose all my money. However, I don't regret being extraverted in the slightest! I believe in living my life out to the fullest, if that wasn't obvious enough from my motto below. Pretty cool animation, right? Thanks, I suffered for it :D Back to the point on energy, I tend to subconsciously synergise my energy with those around me, unless I'm exhausted. That's why who I choose to surround myself with is an important choice as they will inevitably influence my energy.

N - Intuitive

"88% of those with the Intuitive trait say they spend a lot of time thinking about “What if?” scenarios, compared to 63% of those with the Observant trait." ~ 16personalities

As an intuitive, I had a bad habit of living in my head when I was younger. It took many years of practice and focus to remember that as real as it may feel, ultimately, it is only in my mind. It's important for me to not get caught up in such intense thoughts and ground myself. To live in the here and now may be a blessing and/or a curse, but it is definitely an experience. And, what is the point of going through life without actually experiencing it? Worrying about "What if?"s won't get me anywhere. The only way to move forward is to confront life. However, everyone should still dream from time to time. Sometimes, fantasising about "What if" might just be the very thing that revitalises us!

F - Feeling

"People with the Feeling (F) trait follow their hearts and emotions – sometimes without even realizing it. They may show it to different degrees and in different ways, but however they do it, Feeling personality types tend to be caring, compassionate, and warm." ~ 16personalities

My motto below this is already a telltale sign. For those who know me, it goes without saying, I value being true to myself. I'm not the best person. All I seem to do is stumble and fumble. I bumble and grumble. But, I'm not even humble. Yet, I do my best. As cliche as it may sound, I do believe in staying true to myself. Although I may falter in my beliefs and morals, at the end of the day, I know I will find myself back on the right track to become someone that I can accept. No matter what, I strive to be sincere and genuine as can be, in helping myself and in helping others.

P - Prospecting

"Prospecting (P) individuals are much more flexible when it comes to dealing with unexpected challenges. This flexibility helps them seize unexpected opportunities as well...People with this trait hold that life is full of possibilities." ~ 16personalities

Basically, an oppotunist. However, if life gave me lemons, I probably would not make lemonade. On the bright side, I can improvise well and go with the flow easily in times of need. I see all that life has to offer, and I go for it. Doors are always open, you just have to be good at hide-and-seek. But, on the not so bright side, I am a procrastinator. I'm indecisive because I want to construct the best possible outcome. As such, I end up not focusing on a single project and complete nothing apart from daydreaming about completing said project. As life will have it, I am not perfect. Therefore, I must go through the torturous turmoil of learning to let go and simply shooting my shot.
